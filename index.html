San Francisco Crime Changes by Time and Place

This short data story explores how crime patterns in San Francisco shift across hours, years, and neighborhoods. The goal is not just to show where crime appears, but to explain how different visualizations reveal different parts of the same story.

Introduction

San Francisco crime data contains both temporal and spatial structure. Some crimes cluster at particular times of day, while others are concentrated in certain districts or hotspots. Looking at just one chart often hides that complexity. In this story, I use three visualizations to show how crime changes across time and space, and how the choice of visualization shapes what becomes visible.
